Rio Tinto's Diavik diamond mine reaches end of production after 23 years

Rio Tinto has marked the end of production at its Diavik diamond mine in Canada's Northwest Territories, concluding more than two decades of operations that yielded over 150-million carats of rough diamonds.

The mine, which began production in 2003, has been a cornerstone of Canada's diamond mining industry and has contributed significantly to the economic development of the Northwest Territories.

"Diavik has been a remarkable operation that has delivered exceptional value for our shareholders and the communities in which we operate," said Rio Tinto's Chief Executive. "We are proud of the legacy this mine leaves behind, particularly in terms of Indigenous partnerships and environmental stewardship."

The closure plan includes a comprehensive reclamation program that will transform the mine site over the coming years. Rio Tinto has committed to working closely with Indigenous communities and territorial governments throughout the process.

The company said the mine employed approximately 1,100 workers during peak operations and generated billions of dollars in economic activity for Canada.
